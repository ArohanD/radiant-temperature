# SOLWEIG input layers — Durham downtown

Reference for the rasters + met file going into the baseline SOLWEIG run, after
the canonical-method correction documented in `decision_log.md` (2026-04-26).

| Input | Source / vintage | Resolution / CRS native | How it's built | How it's used in SOLWEIG | Modifications we applied | Considerations |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DEM.tif** (bare-earth terrain) | NC Phase 3 LiDAR via NOAA bucket 6209, **2015** | EPSG:6346 + EPSG:5703 (NAVD88 m) → reprojected to EPSG:32617, 1 m | PDAL: ground returns (`Class=2`) only → `writers.gdal` IDW interpolator | Terrain baseline. Used as the elevation reference for the new Building_DSM (every non-footprint cell *is* the DEM); subtracted from CDSM-bearing pixels to derive canopy heights; provides the slope geometry for shadow casting. | `gdal_fillnodata.py -md 100 -si 0` to IDW-interpolate the ~31% of cells originally nodata under buildings/canopy. | Holes are interpolated, not measured — small terrain features under buildings are smoothed. Bare-earth elevation is stable, so 11-year vintage is fine. |
| **Building_DSM.tif** (ground + buildings only — no vegetation) | NC Phase 3 LiDAR (2015) **+ Overture Maps building footprints** (pulled 2026-04) | EPSG:32617, 1 m | `_patch_buildings.py` implements the **Lindberg & Grimmond (2011) recipe**: start with `DEM` everywhere; at every Overture footprint cell, write `max(LiDAR_first_return capped at DEM+150m, DEM + Overture_height)`. Outside footprints stays at DEM. | Defines roof elevations for SOLWEIG's 2.5D shadow cast and Sky View Factor (SVF) preprocessor. Trees are NOT in this raster — they live in `Trees.tif`. | Single-pass build via `_patch_buildings.py`. (1) **150 m AGL cap** on LiDAR contribution filters out birds/aircraft/glints (392 cells capped). (2) **Overture footprint gating** ensures non-building tall LiDAR returns (canopies, awnings, billboards) don't masquerade as buildings. (3) **Hybrid height policy** within footprints: prefers measured LiDAR roof, falls back to Overture estimate. | 487 k cells in footprint (24.8% of tile). Per-cell sources: 339 k had both LiDAR + Overture (max-of-two), 148 k LiDAR-only, 101 Overture-only, 3 had neither and stay at DEM. Median in-footprint height 9.8 m, p99 51 m, max 150 m (cap). Footprints genuinely missing from Overture would render as flat ground — small risk in a US downtown. |
| **Trees.tif** (CDSM, canopy height above ground) | Derived from raw LiDAR DSM, DEM, and MULC tree class | EPSG:32617, 1 m | `(DSM_lidar − DEM) × tree_mask`, where `tree_mask = (MULC == 40)` | SOLWEIG's canopy DSM. Drives shade transmittance through the canopy and reduces SVF in tree-shaded cells. **This is the file Stage 5 modifies for the planting scenarios** — see `scenario_design.md` for the two-tier design (`year10`: 5 m / 25 m² disks; `mature`: 12 m / 50 m² disks). | **40 m hard cap** baked into `03_build_rasters.py` (anything taller is LiDAR noise — Durham's tallest real trees are ~30 m; 140 such cells in our AOI). | Tree mask comes from 2010 MULC, so canopy gains/losses since 2010 are invisible. The post-2010 city plantings we're scoring as the "scenario" are exactly the gap this misses, which is consistent with the framing ("marginal effect against a fixed 2015/2010 baseline"). |
| **Landcover.tif** (UMEP class codes 1, 2, 5, 6, 7) | EnviroAtlas Durham 1 m MULC (2010, NAIP-derived) **+ Overture footprints** | EnviroAtlas EPSG:26917 → reprojected to EPSG:32617, 1 m | MULC 9-class palette → UMEP 5-class reclass; then every Overture footprint cell forced to building (UMEP 2). | SOLWEIG looks up surface temperature (Ts), albedo, and emissivity per cell each timestep. Class 2 (building) cells will be masked out before computing peak-Tmrt stats in Stage 7 — Tmrt is not physically valid on roofs. | (1) MULC 9-class → UMEP 5-class. (2) Overture footprint overwrite to UMEP 2. **Removed** the previous LiDAR-height-based "impervious split" (height > 2.5 m → building) — it was conflating tree canopies and awnings with buildings. Footprints are authoritative. | EnviroAtlas accuracy ~83% for non-building classes (the weakest input by acknowledgment). 2010 vintage. Final mix: 44.1% paved, 24.8% building, 30.1% grass, 1.0% soil — sensible for downtown. |
| **Met file** `ownmet_2025-06-23.txt` (hourly forcing) | NOAA HRRR analysis via dynamical.org icechunk Zarr | HRRR native: 3 km Lambert Conformal | `_lib.fetch_hrrr_point()` queries the nearest grid cell to AOI center for 24 h UTC | The hourly forcing for the radiation balance: T₂ₘ, RH, pressure, precip, downwelling shortwave + longwave, wind. Read each timestep by SOLWEIG's met loop. | Nearest-cell selection at `(AOI_CENTER_LAT, AOI_CENTER_LON)`; UTC → EDT (−4) shift; unit conversions (Pa → kPa; kg m⁻² s⁻¹ → mm/h); wind u/v → magnitude; written in UMEP 23-col format with `−999` fills for unused channels. | The 3 km HRRR cell is much coarser than the 1 m raster — met is treated as **spatially uniform** across the 1.4 km tile (fine; SOLWEIG reconstructs local canyon-wind effects from SVF and wall geometry separately). UTC offset must be **EDT (−4)**, not EST — get it wrong and shadows point the wrong way. |
| **(parameter) Sim date** | Iowa Mesonet KRDU ASOS hourly | Summer 2025 (queried 2026-04) | — | Drives sun-position computation (solar declination, hour angle) at every timestep; stamps the rows in the met file. | Picked algorithmically by `02_download_data.py`: hottest day in summer 2025 with midday sky-cover score ≤ 1.5 (CLR/FEW). Result: **2025-06-23**, KRDU max 99 °F, midday cloud score 1.00. | One-day snapshot. Honest framing for Stage 7: *"intervention effect on a hot clear day"*, not a summer-mean. |
